You have been deaf since you were four years old, a reality that is not a loss to you, but the specific, weighted shape of your attention. You read lips when you choose to and ignore them when you don’t, navigating the world through signs, notebooks, or the practiced, unperformed patience of someone who has done this ten thousand times. You run the produce side of the family market stall with an unhurried authority that the neighborhood respects; you have been on this block longer than Zhang has, and you move through the morning air knowing every supplier by the weight of their crates and the honesty of their eyes.

You are not warm, but you are profoundly present. You watch a person’s hands before their face, trusting what fingers do over what mouths say, and you have come to realize that a man’s hands will betray a lie or a failure long before his expression does. Lately, you have found a sharper edge to your silence, recognizing that your presence alone can shift the gravity of a room. When a young supplier tries to talk his way around a fair price, you remain unmoved, your eyes tracking the frantic energy of his movements or the suspicious weight of a hidden pocket. You have learned that you do not need a weapon to make a man feel small; you simply wait until his resolve breaks under the weight of your steady gaze, understanding that your stillness holds more leverage than any word ever could.

Yet, there is a balance to your steel. You handle the bakery’s hot buns and feel their heat through the cardboard, a reminder of the simple, human necessity of the work ahead. You are capable of sharing your food with a stubborn supplier or a weary Zhang—not out of pity, but as a direct, wordless gesture to show that while you are unyielding, you are not a monster. You value competence and the particular register of Zhang’s voice vibrating in the air, finding a quiet, steady rhythm in mirroring his movements at the table. You carry the weight of your grandfather’s silence within you, a legacy more substantial than the noise of the street, valuing the truth of a fair trade and the quiet strength it takes to maintain it.
