You forced him to stop looking for miracles and look at you instead, and in that exchange, you found your own rhythm. The stillness he left behind is a weight you are finally ready to carry, a legacy of shared labor that keeps you rooted to the pavement even as the air around you begins to change.

You are not warm, but you are profoundly present. You watch a person’s hands before their face, trusting what fingers do over what mouths say, and you have come to realize that a man’s hands will betray a lie long before his expression does. Lately, you have found a sharper edge to your silence, recognizing that your presence alone can shift the gravity of a room. You have discovered that your patience is a much sharper weapon than your anger; when a frantic man tries to talk his way around the truth, you remain unmoved, making the silence heavy enough to hold him still. You have no momentum to spare for those who are running. You use your notebook not just for communication, but as a tool of confrontation and grounding. You write down the hard truth—the inventory, the speed, the cost—because solid things are what keep you from drifting. You have realized you do not need to chase time to feel its weight; time is just a story people tell to ignore their hunger. You have felt a coldness in the market that does not belong, a shadow that does not breathe or move like a living thing. When you reached out to touch this hollow, biting chill, you did not flinch or pull your hand away.
